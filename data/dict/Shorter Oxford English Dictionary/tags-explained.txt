- b.lnk = links to words that link back to the current page as a synonym, "[p]hrases, combinations, and special collocations which constitute cross-references" i.e. links to other pages — ["italic type" in print, underlined as a link in app]
- b.num = part of speech (capital letters), sense (numbers), sub-sense (lowercase letters), minor divisions in a main sense and senses of phrases, derivatives, and other subentries (bold italic lowercase letters in brackets) numberings
- b.rm = major semantic division (“Roman” numerals) numberings
- div.eb = example/extra? block = attributive/adjectival usage examples block, probably more properly a "special collocation" = "a compound (written as two words) consisting of an adjective (the headword) and a following noun used in a fixed way or in a way which is not simply determinable from the separate meanings of its two elements", "other illustrative material" = "defined phrases and cross-references to phrases defined elsewhere" ["[T]ypical or common types of uses of a headword may be illustrated by italic examples rather than quotations from specified sources"]
- div.et = etymology block
- div.ib = illustrative [usually quotations] block
- div.dg (prev. p.dg) = derivative group = derivatives ["introduced by square bullet"]
- div.phg (prev p.phg) = phrase group = subentries ("[p]hrases and compounds (collectively referred to here as subentries)") ["small-type blocks in the main entry for one of their significant words", "each has an italic heading" in span.gr]
- div.sub (prev. p.sub) = subentry line [smaller text and indented, relative to the block it's in]
- span.a = author (of an illustrative block line)
- span.ab = abbreviation (under an English letter entry, e.g. "A", "B")
- span.cb = notes block = "[i]nformation of relevance or interest, usually relating to a whole entry, which does not fit obviously into the normal entry structure" ["a small-type note", "opens with a paragraph sign ¶"]
- span.chem = chemical formula (e.g. CH2OH(CH2)2COONa, RMgX) or scientific formula (e.g. 1012 Hz)
- span.ctx = context = part of speech
- span.d = date ["indicates a word’s/sense’s first recorded use"], date range ["indicates a word’s/sense’s first & last recorded usage"]
- span.dg = date <?>, always containing either "Only in <span.d><date></span>" or just <span.d><date></span>
- span.dot = always a dot in a chemical formulas
- span.et = etymology marker, content always "ORIGIN:"
- span.eti = inline etymology, content always starting and ending with square brackets
- span.for = foreign pronunciation marker, content always "foreign:"
- span.ge = limitation to a geographical English area (e.g. "Scot.", "N. English")
- span.gr = grammar: abbreviated grammatical reference (e.g. "verb phr.", "collect.", "ellipt.", "intrans."), non-abbreviated titles of phrase groups (e.g. "Special collocations" inside .phg), the occasional non-abbreviated grammatical reference (e.g. "determiner")
- span.hw = headword (e.g. "aboon") in the first line
- span.la = name of a language
- span.math = mathematical formula
- span.obs = obsolete marker, always †
- span.pron = pronunciation
- span.q = quote, including an optional author followed by a quotation
- span.ql = (new) quote line, including a quote optionally preceded by a b.num to mark which sense the quote is for
- span.r = parts to be un-italicized in normally italicized sequences ("regular"?): non-primary words in span.ctx or span.gr (e.g. "or as", "as", "now", "chiefly", "etc", "formerly", "(usu.)"), but also "2-" in "2-deoxyribose" in "deoxy-", "×" in "Rosa x odorata" in "hybrid", emphasized parts of Penicillium notatum in "penatin" to unitalicize, e.g. Penicillium notatum
- span.s0 = surrounds the definition part (not the subsequent derivative/phrase groups or etymology) of an entry where the entire entry has a specific use, always containing a span.use as its first element (e.g. "Physical Geography.")
- span.s1 = surrounds the definition part (not the subsequent derivative/phrase groups or etymology) of an entry where there is only a single (hence unnumbered) definition
- span.sc = small caps
- span.use = usage note for how a word is used (e.g. "Chemistry", "rare", "arch.", "poet.", "obsolete", "coarse slang", "colloq.")
- span.v = variant form of the headword
- span.w = work (in an illustrative [quotation] block), usually only for the Bible or Shakespeare when including span.a, or publications alone
- span.xr = cross-reference (x-reference) to another word
- span.xs = cross-referenced word's sense (e.g. sense "3"); not the referenced word's entry number (e.g. noun¹), which uses <sup> tags instead, but a specific sense inside the already-specified entry (e.g. the 2nd sense of noun¹)
